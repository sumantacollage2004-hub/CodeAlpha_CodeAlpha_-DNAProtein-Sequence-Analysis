    Analysis Summary Report

    1. Query Information

    The submitted sequence was processed through BLAST homology search and Smith-Waterman local alignment against the NCBI/UniProt reference database. The analysis includes GC content profiling, ORF detection, and ML-based k-mer similarity scoring.

    2. BLAST Results

    Significant homologs were identified across multiple species, with identity values ranging from ~24% to ~99% depending on evolutionary distance. Top hits show statistically significant E-values well below the threshold of 0.001, confirming genuine homology rather than random similarity.

    3. Smith-Waterman Alignment

    Local alignment was computed using dynamic programming. The alignment score, percent identity, similarity, and gap percentage are reported for the top pairwise comparison. Match: +2, Mismatch: −1, Gap open: −5, Gap extend: −1.

    4. ML k-mer Analysis

    K-mer frequency vectors (k=3) were extracted from query and subject sequences, and cosine similarity was computed as an alignment-free homology metric. This approach is complementary to traditional alignment-based methods.

    5. Phylogenetic Inference

    A simplified neighbor-joining tree was constructed from BLAST identity values. Branch lengths are proportional to evolutionary distance (1 − fractional identity), providing a visual representation of sequence divergence across taxa.

    6. Conclusion

    Combined results from BLAST search, Smith-Waterman alignment, and ML k-mer scoring consistently confirm the query sequence as a conserved gene product with clear homologs across vertebrates and invertebrates.
